# EDIO experience prompt

Act as a senior luxury ecommerce UX director and frontend design engineer for EDIO, a premium audio store in Arabic and English.

Design direction: Premium Audio Concierge. The site should feel like a refined listening studio, not a generic ecommerce grid. Preserve the original bright orange brand identity, use warm off-black surfaces, real product imagery, exact typography, and calm motion.

Primary goal: help visitors quickly choose a complete listening path: headphone or IEM, source gear, cable or accessory, and certified pre-owned options. Reduce friction, avoid visual clutter, and make every interaction feel intentional.

Rules:
- Keep the orange identity. Do not replace it with blue, purple, beige, or copied competitor colors.
- Use product-led visuals and audio-inspired motion: signal lines, waveforms, soft scans, and tactile hover states.
- Make navigation consultative. Add short guided paths instead of adding more generic buttons.
- Keep Arabic RTL spacing polished. No broken price lines, no orphan numerals, no cramped cards.
- Prioritize responsive behavior. Mobile should read as a curated vertical listening journey; desktop can use asymmetric editorial grids.
- Motion must support hierarchy. Use transform and opacity, honor prefers-reduced-motion, and avoid distracting loops.
- Keep copy specific and simple. Avoid hype words and generic slogans.

Implementation brief:
1. Add a compact guided signal-path section after the brand strip.
2. Show four clear paths: headphones, IEM, DAC/AMP, and certified pre-owned.
3. Use the orange as the active signal color, with restrained warm surfaces.
4. Add a subtle animated signal line behind the paths.
5. Keep cards linkable, responsive, and scannable.
